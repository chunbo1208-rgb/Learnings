# Problem Statement
Let $C$ be a positive real number. A positive integer $a$ is called a **good number** if it can be written as $a=xy$, where $x,y$ are positive integers and $|x-y|\le C\sqrt{a}$. List all good numbers in increasing order as $a_1<a_2<\cdots$. Determine the least real number $\lambda=\lambda(C)$ such that there exists a positive integer $M$ with
$$a_{n+1}\le a_n+M\cdot a_n^{\lambda}$$
for every positive integer $n$.
