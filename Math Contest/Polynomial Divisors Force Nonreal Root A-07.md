# Problem Statement
Let $n$ and $k$ be positive integers with $k<n$. Let $P(x)$ be a real polynomial of degree $n$ with nonzero constant term and no repeated roots. Suppose that for any real numbers $a_0,a_1,\dots,a_k$, if the polynomial
$$a_kx^k+\cdots+a_1x+a_0$$
divides $P(x)$, then the product $a_0a_1\cdots a_k$ is zero. Prove that $P(x)$ has a nonreal root.
