# Problem Statement
Let $m,n$ be positive integers. Let $P_1,P_2$ be nonconstant integer-coefficient polynomials in $m$ variables, and let $Q_1,Q_2$ be nonconstant integer-coefficient polynomials in $n$ variables. Suppose that for any integers $a_1,\dots,a_m,b_1,\dots,b_n$,
$$P_1(a_1,\dots,a_m)\ne Q_1(b_1,\dots,b_n)$$
implies that
$$\frac{P_2(a_1,\dots,a_m)-Q_2(b_1,\dots,b_n)}{P_1(a_1,\dots,a_m)-Q_1(b_1,\dots,b_n)}$$
is an integer. Prove that there exists a one-variable polynomial $R$ with rational coefficients such that
$$P_2=R(P_1),\qquad Q_2=R(Q_1).$$
